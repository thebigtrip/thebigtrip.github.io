June 5, 2018

Last week I visited The Hermitage in Three Rivers, Michigan. It was a four day retreat with a focus on rest and prayer. It gave space and time to reflect on the last season of ministry, and to mark the transition to this Summer with all it’s plans and possibilities. The time was very good. Each morning I gathered with others for Morning Prayers. I met with one of the directors there, David, for about an hour to talk and pray together through how God was leading me through this time. And then the days were spent in a rhythm of rest, reading, meals, and walks around the grounds. One of the big themes was just slowing down, like riding a bike and working down through the gears, feathering the brakes. It takes time to slow down. And then, after a couple of days of that, I found, wonderfully, it had happened. I felt…rested. My mind seemed less scattered. I could read for more than 20 minutes without falling asleep (and without coffee). I felt ready…to get moving again. Which is good because there will be a lot of being on the move this summer. One of the delightful surprises was digging into a book that had been pointed out to us recently. In Search of Deep Faith: A Pilgrimage Into the Beauty, Goodness and Heart of Christianity, Jim Belcher, who is a pastor, shares of taking a sabbatical break with wife and four children, and traveling to Oxford as a pilgrimage of learning and spiritual growth. (Here’s a nice video on the part of their trip where they visit C.S. Lewis’ Oxford home: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XQnJ9BQgzXs). The parallels with our own summer plans gave the chance to think through how God has prompted and guided us to a similar kind of journey this summer. This week we’re home. Catching a couple of baseball games. Making final preparations and packing. Tying up loose ends. Yet still resting, too. And getting ready to gear up in a whole different way. The next leg will be to Cedar Campus in Michigan’s U.P. Thanks for your ongoing prayers on our behalf. - Darrin

Pastoral Sabbatical 2018 Overview

Description:
The theme developed for the the Sabbatical comes from the friendship and writings of “The Inklings,” a group featuring most prominently C.S. Lewis and J.R.R. Tolkien. The key idea is to explore what in their friendship helped them to not only stay faithful as followers of Christ, but to do so with such abundant imagination and overflowing creativity that gave, and continues to give through their writings, joy and hope to so many.
This sabbatical involves spending time with their works and those who’ve studied them, as well as seeking out the sources of their rich friendship in travel, learning and adventure. And doing all this so that our joy and delight is deepened in sharing the Gospel and sharing our lives. Our original Sabbatical plans have had to be somewhat flexible as we’ve looked to turn them into reality. Some things we thought would work great turned out not to.
The family camp we’d looked into was booked by the time we learned of the grant’s approval, for example.
But other possibilities opened up before us that fit the themes and purpose of the Sabbatical beautifully. Some of this is still in development, but we can now paint a fairly full picture of what we anticipate the summer looking like.
——————————————————————-
Timeline and Plans:
May 20 - Pentecost - Last Sunday of leading worship prior to Sabbatical

May 21-June 7 - Theme: “The Shire: Preparing”
We’ll be mostly at home for these days, catching our breath, tidying, and getting ready for the summer ahead. Pastor Darrin will take a 3-day spiritual retreat to The Hermitage (Three Rivers) for a time of rest and prayer. And we’ll look to connect with family and friends ahead of our weeks of travel.

June 8-15 - Theme: “Rivendell (Part I): Explore, Play, Rest, Friendships” For these weeks we’ll get ready and head as a family to Cedar Campus in Michigan’s Upper peninsula. We’ll begin some of our summer reading together of Lewis and Tolkien’s key works, and for the last few days of our time there some good friends of ours will come to spend a few days with us.

June 16-20 - Theme: “The Road Towards Lothlorien: Gathering Wisdom”
Pastor Darrin will go with friend and colleague Pastor John Lee and his wife, Mary Jo, to Wheaton College (Chicago) for a conference of the C.S. Lewis Society. The conference’s theme is “Communicating the Gospel C.S. Lewis Style.”

June 21-July 21 - Theme: “Lothlorien: Study the Sources, Friendships”
The Compagner family will travel (accompanied by Sarah’s mother) to England. Our flights will take us to Amsterdam where we’ll take advantage of a couple days to explore some sites of family and historical interest. We’ll then go via train and ship to the UK, beginning with some days in the Cornwall region (South West England) whose seascapes inspired some of Lewis’ and Tolkiens’ writings. We’ll then head to Oxford (image below left) for a few weeks (where we will reconnect with the Lees), and Pastor Darrin will take a class at Oxford University (the course’s title: “Heresy, Crusade and Inquisition in Southern France, 1150-1250”).
We look forward to walking the town, visiting C.S. Lewis’ Oxford home (“The Kilns”), and visiting the pub (“The Eagle and Child”) where the Inklings gathered to share their readings and conversations. Various day trips to London or other sites are likely. We’ll conclude our time in England with a few days traveling North to the Lakes District.

July 22-August 3 - Theme: “Minas Tirith: Travel, Adventure, Mountains”
We’ll travel via train to France. Both Lewis and Tolkien fought in World War I, and we plan to visit some of the key historical locations that shaped their lives and writings in northern France. We’ll then work our way south towards Switzerland. We have a tentative visit to Taize (a Christian monastic community that welcomes visitors from all over the world) in our plans. We’ll then head to Switzerland for time of hiking and exploring some of the territory that helped to inspire Tolkien’s creation of Middle Earth.

August 4-18 - Theme: “Rivendell (Part II): Explore, Play, Rest”
We’ll stay at Credo Castle, a Christian ministry that will serve as a base for hikes and exploring in Switzerland. Credo sits at the base of Lauterbrunnen Valley (image below right) which was the key inspiration for Tolkien’s Rivendell.

August 19-25 - Theme: “Back to the Shire: Returning Slowly”
Arriving home and catching our breath, Pastor Darrin has (tentative) plans to visit the Hermitage for a closing time of prayer and thanksgiving for the sabbatical. Then we’ll resume life and responsibilities, returning to work on August 26 and to preaching and leading worship on September 2.

——————————————————————-
This all feels somewhat like a dream to us. We are very grateful for Blythefield’s Sabbatical Policy, for the council’s encouragement, and the congregation’s blessing in making this all possible. We’re very grateful for the grant through Clergy Renewal Program that is providing funds to make travel together as a family possible in ways we could barely have otherwise imagined.
And we’re truly hopeful that this time of rest, travel, study, friendship, and adventure will not only nourish us as a family, but will overflow in surprising gifts and blessings for you as our church family, too.
